How to read this tracker

This tracker turns live data and current events into a 1–10 risk level for each "story" (a macro risk theme). Nothing here is a forecast or financial advice — the levels are a transparent, rules-based judgement of how much attention each risk warrants right now.

Plain vs Expert (the button top-right)

Plain
The default. Plain-English names, a one-line "what it is and why it matters" under each story, and a state-of-the-world summary up top. The summary line and the "what changed" list are generated automatically from the scores, so they always match the numbers.
Expert
The full view: original terminology, the tail-weighted aggregation note, and every panel's technical framing. Nothing is hidden in Plain mode — Expert just adds the jargon and the methodology detail. Your choice is remembered on this device.

The risk level & bands

Level (1–10)
Each story's headline score. Higher = more attention warranted. It starts from an analyst-set base level and is nudged by the indicators below.
Sections
The 21 stories are grouped into five mechanism-based sets — Funding & Leverage, Credit/Consumers & Valuations, Sovereigns & the Monetary Order, Geoeconomics & Trade Weapons, and Physical & Resource Limits — so stories that move for the same reason sit together and each set's average means something. Hover a set name for its full title.
Bands
Levels group into five bands: Low (1–2), Moderate (3–4), Elevated (5–6), High (7–8), Severe (9–10). The colour follows the band.
Gauge bar
The horizontal bar under each story fills to its level and is coloured by band — a quick visual of where it sits.

The trend triangle & sparkline

▲ / ▼ / ●
▲ red = the level rose since the comparison date; ▼ green = it fell; ● flat = unchanged. "new" means there isn't enough history yet.
vs <date>
The date the trend is measured against (about a week earlier), so you see direction, not just today's value.
Sparkline
The little line next to a story is its level over recent runs — the shape of its path.

The indicator table

Each story is driven by a handful of indicators — the measurable signals that move its level.

Indicator
One measurable signal feeding the story (a market price, an economic series, or a current-events status).
Value
The latest reading. A number for data indicators (e.g. a spread or price), or a 0/1/2 status for event indicators.
Band (0/1/2)
Each indicator is bucketed: 0 = benign, 1 = watch, 2 = stress, using its thresholds. This is what the engine acts on.
Δ (delta)
How much this indicator is currently pushing the story's level up (+) or down (−) versus its normal baseline. At baseline it contributes 0.
Cadence
daily = refreshed from a live feed every run; event = updated when something happens (AI-rated or human-set), not on a clock.
As of
The date of the underlying reading, so you know how fresh it is.
Source
Where the number comes from, as a clickable link: FRED/Market = automatic live feed; Agent = AI-rated against a written rubric; Baseline = currently using the analyst's starting value (no live update yet).

The panels

Heat map
All stories as colour-coded tiles for a two-second scan. Click a tile to jump to that story.
Countdown bar
At the top: a live countdown to the next scheduled data refresh and AI agent run (the agent runs Monday morning UTC, capturing the weekend). Times are the schedule; GitHub may run a few minutes late.
Data health
How many live feeds updated vs fell back to baseline, when the AI agent last ran, and the overall trend — a trust check at a glance.
Scout
Watches credible feeds for developments the tracker doesn't already capture and proposes additions (re-rate existing / coverage gap / new risk) with a materiality rating. It only proposes — a human approves.
Agent review
The AI rating agent's worklist. Each row separates the FACT (the load-bearing measured datum + primary source) from the rule-applied rating, so the meter is driven by facts, not vibes. Where authoritative sources disagree, the conflict is shown as Dissent and annotated rather than silently resolved. Confidence/applied/held/overridden and reasoning + sources are shown; low-confidence calls are flagged, not applied. Readings outside an indicator's plausible range are caught, marked auto-corrected, and held at the last trusted value.
Consistency checks
Deterministic whole-picture cross-checks that run after scoring. They flag ratings the rest of the board contradicts \u2014 e.g. a chokepoint rated "closed" while crude oil stays benign, or a positive China PPI when the story tracks deflation. These are review flags, not auto-corrections.
Validation
Two honest tests. Calibration replays documented stress episodes (2008/2020/2022/2023) through the live engine and shows whether the overall rises and separates from a calm baseline (market indicators only, so a lower bound). Dynamic range shows the overall now spans ~4.7-9.0: it reaches High/Severe only under broad, correlated stress. Sensitivity ranks which indicators most move their story/set/overall, i.e. where a wrong fact matters most.
Backtest
Replays real 2020 & 2022 market data through the engine to show it responds sensibly to stress. Only the market indicators vary (judgement ones are held at baseline), so it validates mechanics, not predictions.
Background
An expandable plain-language summary of each story, with a link to the full written brief (PDF).

How the number is made (and who controls it)

Scoring
level = base level + the sum of each indicator's weighted delta, clamped to 1–10. Every move is arithmetic and auditable — no AI sets a number directly.
The AI's role
The agent only sets inputs (it reads current events and maps them onto fixed rubrics). The maths is deterministic, and a human override always wins.
Aggregates
Per-Set scores are averages of their story levels. Overall is tail-weighted: it blends the breadth average with the worst cluster (top third of stories) and adds a premium when many stories are High at once, so a broad correlated shock registers instead of being averaged away.
